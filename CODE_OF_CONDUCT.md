# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and professional environment for everyone who interacts with this project, including contributors, maintainers, users, and community members.

We expect all participants to treat others with dignity and courtesy, regardless of background, identity, experience level, or perspective.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and considerate in all interactions
- Communicate constructively and professionally
- Welcome differing viewpoints and feedback
- Focus on the merits of ideas rather than the people involved
- Be honest, helpful, and collaborative
- Respect privacy, boundaries, and confidentiality where applicable

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of behavior that is not acceptable include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation
- Offensive, derogatory, or demeaning language
- Personal attacks or abusive comments
- Trolling, baiting, or disruptive conduct
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Publishing private information without permission
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project-related spaces and interactions, including but not limited to:

- Issue discussions
- Pull requests
- Code reviews
- Project documentation
- Chat or community channels
- Direct interactions related to the project

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may take reasonable action in response to behavior that violates this Code of Conduct. This may include:

- A private warning
- Temporary suspension from participation
- Permanent removal from project-related spaces
- Blocking access to project resources where appropriate

Maintainers will make enforcement decisions in good faith and based on the circumstances of each case.

## Reporting Concerns

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the project maintainers through the appropriate private contact method.

Reports should include as much relevant context as possible, including:

- What happened
- When and where it happened
- Who was involved, if known
- Any supporting evidence or screenshots, if available

## No Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who raises a concern in good faith is not tolerated.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is a project-specific policy inspired by common open-source community standards.
